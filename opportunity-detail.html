Wednesday, May 29, 2016

Special needs students and youth volunteers learn from each other in Rosie’s Class

Meet Daily Point of Light Award honoree Rosie Arbittier. Read her story and nominate an outstanding volunteer, family or organization as a Point of Light.

As a child, Rosie Arbittier, participated in walkathons sponsored by her local synagogue and placed flags on soldiers’ graves on Veterans Day. That spirit of volunteerism continued into her teens, when she started tutoring special needs students at her middle school in York, Pennsylvania.

The experience was an eye-opener.

“I began to see how isolated these kids were,” says Rosie, now 17. “They were told not to talk to me unless I talked to them first. They weren't allowed to audition to be in a school play or to try out for a team. They spent their whole day with the same students and it was obvious that they just weren’t getting enough opportunities to socialize.”

Org/Group name

Greenhouse Rehab and Compost Management

Join This Opportunity
t g+ f Share via

Summary

Fresh Future Farm is a huge endeavor that intends to have a major impact on our community. We want you to be part of it, not just because we need you, but because if we build this together, the results will be amazing. There are many ways to volunteer and to be part of Fresh Future Farm. We can use help around the farm on Fridays and Saturdays and 3-hour shifts are available throughout the day. Greenhouse rehabilitation and compost management positions will require manual labor and shoes that you don’t mind getting dirty. Please bring your own work gloves if possible, the Farm has a small supply for those that do not. Rehab and upkeep normally involves general repairs, cleaning, compost rotation and mixing, and transporting compost with wheelbarrows to our beds and greenhouses.

Requirements

Volunteers must be at least 18 years of age or 15 with a parent's signed permission..

Number of Volunteers needed : 5-10

Minimum age for volunteers : 15

Desired Skills : Public Relations, Visual Communication, Education.

Location

Fresh Future Farm 2008 Success Street, North Charleston, SC, Ph. (815) 300-1100 FAX. (815) 300-1100
